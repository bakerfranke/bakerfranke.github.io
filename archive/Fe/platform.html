What was the problem with this year's student government? Well, there were a few, but I have solutions, answers and a direction to take student government. Right now Student Government is somewhat akin to the Millennium Falcon (Han Solo's ship). It has the ability to make the Kessel run in under twelve parsecs, but at the present moment it is "a hunk of junk" (Princess Leia to Han). In other words, Student Government once was, and still can be a well run organization, but some renovations and new parts are needed.
ne of the major problems this year was that the assembly meetings were not run well at all. (No offense Zack, and Dan). The president must be at least remotely familiar with our school's constitution, and "Robert's Rules of Order." Having worked with this year's cabinet in an effort to revamp the student handbook, I became increasingly familiar with the constitution, and being Student Senate Head, a Senate representative for three years, I am also well versed in "Robert's Rules of Order." Most of you saw this year that I am capable of running the meetings, during our interim stints. If elected efficient meetings will be a constant. Folks, this does not mean boredom. I will work to make meetings interesting, but to do that we can not get caught up in confusion about procedure. I won't let it happen.
nother problem, well not really a problem, but a concern of mine is that I hear so many good ideas for proposals just walking around school, and most of them never materialize. As President I would institute the "suggestion session" in Student government-A five-minute period once in a while where people would just shout out ideas. The secretary would write them down, and then I would post them on the Student Government bulletin board, incase anybody wanted to take the idea and write it up.
ne of the reasons that this year's Presidents probably had so much difficulty was because neither Zack nor Dan really had a chance to get in the flow. Both came into the cabinet amidst a torrent of projects already underway, and neither had time to adjust. Being a member of the cabinet this year I am already in the flow. I know what I'm doing. If elected Student Government would have jump-start at the end of this year, and I'd just keep it rolling into next year. I know what it takes to get this government going, and if elected a period of adjustment wouldn't be necessary. One of the most successful Presidents in Parker history, Greg Feldman, was Student Senate Head before becoming President. I believe that part of the reason that he was so successful was that he knew what to do as soon as he was elected, and got things going right away. I believe that the same would happen to me.
lso, I know for a fact that communication between the president and the administration this year (until very recently) was virtually non-existent (Zack and Dan see Paragraph 1, line 2). Not only am I more than willing to discuss issues with the Administration, but I am not intimidated either. I am willing to fight (and I mean fight) for students' rights, beliefs, and proposals.
astly, this year's Student Government had no direction - no purpose. Student Government is a place where students have the power to change the school. The way those changes are made cannot be through strict procedure; it's not conducive to progressive thought. I would like to bring to Student Government what I brought to Senate - a loose atmosphere that gets things done. If things get rowdy (as they did a few times this year) I believe that I have the ability to control the situation. Again this doesn't have to be done by strict procedure. So let's have a looser government that produces, rather than a government that keeps trying to tighten its grip as all of the creativity slips through its fingers.
I know what it takes to make Student Government Work
Just give me the chance to show you what I know
