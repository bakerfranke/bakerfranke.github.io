Law School Notebook Program

The Law School and the Kellogg Graduate School of Management have teamed up to offer an excellent Dell notebook computer purchasing program at a price well-below current retail costs. The law school requires all students to have access to a computer and, although you are not required to purchase one of the following computers, we strongly recommend that you do so if you do not already have a comparable machine. The following laptops comply with Northwestern University computing standards, and they have the combination of power, reliability, and service that you will need for your three years at the Law School.

Beginning on Monday, July 6, the following Dell notebook computers will be available for purchase by all Northwestern law students at the prices indicated. Students will purchase the computers directly from Dell, and we will follow with detailed ordering instructions prior to the July 6 date. We will include a link on this page at some point in the near future where you can order directly from Dell via the Web. Check this page for updated information as July 6 draws near.

You may also obtain NU Net Installer (which includes Netscape, Eudora and other internet resource tools) and LEXISand WESTLAW connection software (the two principal online legal research databases) from the Law School upon arrival inAugust at no charge. Northwestern will assist with connectivity to the University and Law School systems and providesoftware support for Microsoft Office 97 for students with these Dell laptops or other computers that comply with currentuniversity computing guidelines.

A detailed listing of university specifications is located at http://www.nwu.edu/it/guidelines/cns.html. We will also act as the first point of advice as to whether you need to invoke the warranty if you purchase one of the above Dell laptop computers.We will not offer support for computers which do not comply with minimum university guidelines.

To finance a computer purchase, Northwestern University will provide to law students a one-time loan of up to $3500 tocover the cost of computers, printers, and software purchased after May 1, 1998. In order to receive a computer loan,students must present all receipts for a completed purchase or an itemized statement from an anticipated computer vendor tothe financial aid office. Please note that you are not required to purchase a Dell computer in order to obtain a computer loan.We hope that you will take advantage of this attractive offer. More detailed purchase information will follow prior to July 6.
